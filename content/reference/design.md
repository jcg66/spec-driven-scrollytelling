To solidify the **"System 3: The Action Layer"** theme, we need to ensure the design feels like a high-performance instrument—not a toy. The goal is "Technical Elegance": a mix of deep-space minimalism and data-rich overlays.

Here is the design breakdown by section, focusing on the visual "language" we will use.

---

## 🎨 Global Design Tokens (The "Vibe")
* **The "Texture":** We’ll use a subtle **Digital Grain** or "Noise" overlay across the entire site. This prevents the dark background from looking flat and gives it a tactile, "premium hardware" feel.
* **The "Motion Curve":** Avoid bouncy, cartoonish movements. Use **Exponential Decel** (fast start, very smooth slow-down) to make every scroll feel intentional and heavy.
* **The "Glass" Effect:** For UI elements, use **Ultra-Dark Glassmorphism**. Elements should look like polished obsidian with a slight 10% transparency, letting the background "glow" peek through.

---

## 🖼️ Section-Specific Design Summaries

### 1. The Spark (Hero Section)
* **Visual Focus:** Total isolation. 
* **Styling:**
    * The **Prompt Field** should not have a visible border. It's just a blinking cursor in the void.
    * As the text types, the letters should have a slight **"Glow Bleed"** (a faint blue halo) that mimics old CRT monitors but in high resolution.
    * **The Core:** A 3D sphere made of thousands of tiny, orbiting data-points. It doesn't look like a solid object; it looks like a "cloud of thought."

### 2. The Deconstruction (Bento Grid)
* **Visual Focus:** Organization out of chaos.
* **Styling:**
    * **The Grid:** Use "Micro-Borders." Instead of thick dividers, use 0.5px faint silver lines that only light up when the user's scroll triggers a specific "Thought Block."
    * **Node Connections:** Thin, glowing "Neural Filaments" (think spider-web thin) should physically connect the Brain Core to the active grid tiles.
    * **Typography:** Labels like `TASK_ID: 8802` should be in a tiny, 10px monospaced font, tucked into the corners of the tiles to look like technical metadata.

### 3. The Digital Eye (Visual Grounding)
* **Visual Focus:** The "Computer Vision" HUD (Heads-Up Display).
* **Styling:**
    * **The Browser Window:** A "Dark Mode" stylized browser with rounded corners.
    * **Bounding Boxes:** These are the stars here. They should be **Phosphor Green outlines** with "crosshair" corners. 
    * **Scanning Effect:** A faint horizontal "Laser Line" should periodically sweep down the window, highlighting elements as it passes, showing that the AI is "scanning" the pixels in real-time.

### 4. The Execution Loop (The Terminal)
* **Visual Focus:** Rhythmic velocity.
* **Styling:**
    * **The Log Stream:** Use a "Gradient Fade" on the text. The most recent action at the bottom is bright Phosphor Green; as lines move up, they dim and turn grey, eventually dissolving into the background.
    * **Action Pings:** Every time the AI "clicks," a circular ripple effect (like a drop in water) should radiate from the point of contact on the screen.
    * **Sidebar Styling:** A vertical "Progress Rail" on the far right that fills up as the agent completes the multi-step loop.

### 5. The Outcome (Final Resolution)
* **Visual Focus:** Calm and Clarity.
* **Styling:**
    * **The "Success Orb":** The chaotic data-cloud from Section 1 finally settles into a perfect, glowing sphere. It pulses slowly, like a "breathing" light.
    * **Whitespace:** Increase the padding significantly here. We want the user to feel the "relief" of a completed task.
    * **The Summary Card:** A clean, centered card with a high-contrast white border. Use a "checkered" background pattern (like a transparency map in Photoshop) to signify that the task is "rendered" and finished.

---

## 💎 Design Detail: The "Agentic Cursor"
Instead of a standard white arrow, our "Agentic Cursor" will be a **Custom Pointer**:
* A small, hollow circle.
* When it "clicks," the circle should momentarily contract and flash.
* It should move with a slight "Lag and Snap" (meaning it moves smoothly, then snaps perfectly to the center of a button), emphasizing machine precision.
