The homepage is the canonical public story route.

It starts with an intent, turns that intent into a workflow, grounds the workflow in the interface, executes the plan, and closes with a concrete outcome.

This is the action layer in motion: a system that does not stop at language, but moves through perception, reasoning, action, and correction until the work is complete.

## Spark

The story opens in isolation. A request appears as a prompt, and the page frames that request as the first step into the system of action.

The scene is deliberately spare. The prompt is the signal, the glow is the response, and the rest of the page waits for the agent to begin.

## Deconstruction

The request becomes structure. The agent separates the goal into parts, constraints, and tasks that can be handled one step at a time.

This is the planner-grounder split at work: the higher-level plan defines what must happen, while the grounded steps keep the work tied to what the interface can actually support.

## Digital Eye

The narrative shifts to visual grounding. The agent reads the interface, follows the visible state, and uses that context to stay aligned with the task.

The point is not just to look at a page. The point is to interpret what is on the page well enough to act on it with precision.

## Execution Loop

The story moves into action. The agent clicks, checks feedback, and keeps adapting as the environment changes.

```viz:event-log
{"entries":[{"level":"info","message":"Action: inspect the task board","detail":"The agent looks for deadline conflicts and mismatched dependencies."},{"level":"success","message":"Observation: conflicts detected","detail":"The board and the calendar are no longer aligned."},{"level":"info","message":"Next: sync the final update","detail":"The loop continues until the plan converges."}]}
```

This is the execution loop: act, observe, correct. The agent keeps the work moving until the result settles into place.

## Outcome

The page closes with a completed result and a calm resolution state.

The outcome matters because it makes the process legible. The visitor sees not just that the work finished, but how a system of action can bridge intent and completion.
